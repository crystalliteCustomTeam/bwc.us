6 Essential Types of Novel Editing Every Author Should Know About.

By BWC Team in 2023

When authors set out to publish their novels, the novel editing process may seem daunting. After countless hours of hard work and dedication, it can be difficult to relinquish control to book editors. But this is a vital step in the book publishing journey that must not be skipped!

The best book editors are experienced professionals who review manuscripts/novels for accuracy, clarity, and consistency to make them ready for publication. They can help authors identify and correct errors, provide feedback on the overall book structure, and develop the book’s style and tone. In short, novel editing services are essential when it comes to ensuring that a book is of publishable quality. But what types of book editing should authors know about?

Stay tuned to learn about the different types of novel editing.

What Do Novel Editors Do

?

“Novel editors are responsible for analyzing and refining books, stories and other written works before they are published by book publishers. They review manuscripts to identify inconsistencies, errors or inaccuracies in spelling, grammar, structure, style and content.”

A novel editor is responsible for reviewing manuscripts, doing a content review, copy-editing or proofreading, checking factual accuracy and consistency in the text, ensuring that grammar and punctuation are correct, and suggesting changes to improve readability. Novel editors will also provide guidance on plot structure, characterization, and other narrative elements to ensure the story flows smoothly.

Additionally, best book editors may also suggest ways to make a book more marketable such as introducing new characters or changing the title.

How To Format a Novel to Submit to Editors

?

Submitting a novel to a book editor is daunting for many authors. While having an excellent story can be the most important element for success, guidelines must be followed to ensure a smooth process. Knowing how to format your novel properly will not only make the submission process easier, but it can also help to create a professional impression of your work.

The first step in formatting your novel should include setting up page margins. A common size is one inch on all sides, although some publishers may require different measurements, so double-check their specific requirements before submitting. It is also important to use double spacing and 12-point font throughout your document, as this helps editors quickly read through large amounts of material.

Additionally, ensure that each chapter has its own page and begins halfway down the page with no extra spaces or lines between chapters.

Here Are The 6 Essential Types of Novel Editing

.

For aspiring authors, the process of writing a novel can be an overwhelming experience. After all, it takes hours upon hours of dedication to craft a narrative that is engaging and captivating. But once a manuscript is complete, crafting a well-written story isn’t over yet. Novel editing services are essential in creating a polished piece of literature that readers can enjoy for years to come.

With so many types of novel editing out there, it’s important to know which ones are essential for achieving the best results. Here are the six essential types of novel editing every author should consider:

1.

Developmental Editing

.

Developmental editing is the first type of book editing services and is typically done before the book is even written. During this stage, the editor works with the author to develop the story's characters, plot, and setting. This type of editing can be beneficial for authors who need help to get their ideas down on paper.

2.

Line Editing

.

Line editing is the second type of novel editing that offers numerous novel editing benefits to authors, and is typically done after the book has been written. During this stage, the editor goes through the manuscript line by line and corrects grammar, spelling, and punctuation errors. Line editing can be very helpful for authors who want to ensure that their book is free from any typos or other errors.

3.

Copy Editing

.

Copy editing is the third type of novel editing and is typically done after the book has been written. During this stage, the copy editor reviews the manuscript and corrects grammar, spelling, and punctuation errors. Copy editing can be very helpful for authors who want to ensure that their book is free from any typos or other errors.

4. Proofreading

Proofreading is the fourth type of novel editing and is typically done after the book has been written. During this stage, a book editor reviews the manuscript and looks for any grammar, spelling, or punctuation errors. Proofreading can be very helpful for authors who want to ensure that their book is free from any typos or other errors.

5. Substantive or Full Editing

Substantive or full editing for novels requires a comprehensive understanding of the plot, characters, and settings to ensure the story is well-crafted. Substantive editors look at the overall structure of a novel, including plot development, pacing, characterization, dialogue, and scene setting. Full editing goes one step further by ensuring that all the necessary elements are included in the manuscript and that they work together cohesively.

6. Evaluation Editing

Evaluation editing for novels is a crucial part of the writing and publishing process. It ensures that a book is ready for publication with no errors, inconsistencies, or weaknesses in the text, structure, and format. Evaluation editors put every page under the microscope to ensure it accurately conveys what the author intends and meets readers’ expectations. Whether it’s a work of fiction or nonfiction, evaluation editing can help authors ensure that their book is the best it can possibly be.

How Many Editors Do I Need For My Novel

?

If you are a new author looking to self-publish your novel, one of the first questions that may come up is: How many editors do I need for my novel? The answer to this question will depend on the type of book you have written and its overall length.

A longer novel with complex plot lines and characters may require more than one editor to help ensure accuracy and consistency throughout the entire manuscript. A shorter, simpler story might need one editor. If you are still determining exactly how many editors your project requires, consider seeking advice from other authors in your genre or consult a professional writing service. They will advise you on which type of editor best suits your book's needs and budget.

How Much Do Novel Editors Charge

?

Novel editors are one of the most important people in any writer’s life. They help ensure that the novel you write is not only error-free but will also be able to engage readers and leave them with a lasting impression. But how much do these editors charge? It’s important to understand these professionals' costs before choosing novel editors for your project.

The cost of hiring a novel editor depends on several factors, such as the length of your novel and its complexity. Generally speaking, editors will charge by word count or per hour, with rates ranging from around $0.01 to $0.10 per word and hourly rates ranging from $20 - $50 USD per hour, respectively.

What Are Editors Looking for In a Good Novel

?

Good novels capture the imagination and transport readers to faraway places. They tell stories that linger in the mind long after a reader has finished reading them. So, what exactly are editors looking for when they review manuscripts?

Editors want a compelling story with characters that readers can emotionally connect to. The plot should be original and unpredictable, taking the reader on an exciting journey. They also look for well-crafted dialogue and believable conflicts between characters to keep the story interesting and engaging.

Good grammar, correct spelling, and proper punctuation are essential elements of a novel; editors carefully examine every detail before making any publication decisions.

Finally, editors seek out writers with unique styles – something that sets their work apart from other fiction.

What Do Editors Look for When Deciding to Accept a Fiction Novel for Publication

?

When deciding whether or not to accept a fiction novel for publication, editors have a few key considerations they need to consider.

  • First, they analyze the author's writing style and determine if it is good enough to merit publication. They look for evidence of strong grammar and syntax and interesting plot lines and characters that draw in readers.
  • Additionally, editors evaluate the potential of the book in terms of its commercial success, including looking at word-of-mouth buzz about the novel or analyzing how similar books have done in the past.
  • Editors also review any prior work from the author, such as published articles or awards won for other pieces of literature. This allows them to get a sense of their writing ability and experience with publishing industry standards.
