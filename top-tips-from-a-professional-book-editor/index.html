Mistakes To Avoid When Self-Editing: Top Tips From A Professional Book Editor

By BWC Team in 2023

Writing is a journey, and part of that journey involves self-editing. Whether you are an author or a writer, the process of self-editing is important for polishing your work before it reaches readers' hands. To shed light on this task, we've sought advice from a professional book editor. In this comprehensive guide, we'll explore the dos and don'ts of self-editing, share expert insights, and provide real-life success stories to inspire and empower you in your writing journey.

Mistakes To Avoid When Self-Editing

Avoiding Rushed Editing

  1. Not skipping the editing phase: Don't underestimate the importance of editing. Skipping this step can lead to embarrassing errors and diminish the quality of your work.
  1. Not editing immediately after writing: Avoid editing while your writing is still fresh in your mind. Give it time to "cool off" so you can approach it objectively.
  1. Not relying solely on spell checkers: Spell checkers are helpful, but they can't catch everything. Don't rely solely on them; invest time in manual editing as well.

Over Editing and Perfectionism

  1. Knowing when to stop editing: Overediting can strip your work of its authenticity. Recognize when it's time to stop tweaking and start sharing your writing with the world.
  1. Avoiding rewriting entire sections needlessly: Sometimes, writers can get caught in a cycle of rewriting the same section repeatedly. Trust your initial instincts and make edits thoughtfully.
  1. Embracing imperfections: Perfection is unattainable. Understand that your work will never be flawless, and that's okay. It's the imperfections that make your writing unique.

Ignoring Feedback

Seeking input from others: Don't work in isolation. Share your work with beta readers or critique partners who can provide valuable feedback.

Not taking constructive criticism personally: Feedback is meant to help you improve, not criticize you personally. Embrace it as an opportunity to grow as a writer.

Balancing feedback with your vision: While feedback is essential, remember that your writing is ultimately your voice. Use feedback as a guide but stay true to your creative vision.

Neglecting the Big Picture

  1. Focusing only on small details: It's easy to get lost in the particulars of editing. Remember to step back and assess your work's overall impact.
  1. Forgetting the story's overall impact: Every word should contribute to your story's impact. Ensure that your writing serves your larger narrative purpose.
  1. Neglecting character consistency and development: Character arcs should be consistent and satisfying. Don't forget to review and refine your characters' journeys.

Top Tips from a Professional Book Editor

Tips for Self-Editing Success

A professional book editor such as Book Writing Cube yielded a variety of self-editing tips. Here are five valuable pieces of advice to enhance your self-editing journey:

  1. Take Your Time: The editor emphasized the importance of patience. Don't rush through the editing process. Give your writing some distance before revisiting it, ideally after a few days or weeks. This fresh perspective will help you spot errors more effectively.
  1. Create a Checklist: Develop a personalized editing checklist tailored to your writing goals and weaknesses. Include items like grammar, clarity, plot consistency, and character development. Having a structured plan will keep you on track.
  1. Read Aloud: Reading your work aloud is a powerful technique. It helps you catch awkward phrasing, missing words, and other issues that might be missed when reading silently. It also aids in assessing the flow of your writing.
  1. Seek Feedback: Don't shy away from seeking feedback from others. Constructive criticism can provide invaluable insights and fresh perspectives. However, remember that not all feedback is equal; use it to refine your work, but stay true to your vision.
  1. Know When to Stop: Recognize that perfection is elusive. There comes a point when you must stop editing and start sharing your work. Trust your instincts, and remember that your writing will never be entirely flawless.
