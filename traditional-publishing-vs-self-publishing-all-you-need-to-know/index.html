Traditional Publishing vs. Self-Publishing: All You Need To Know

By BWC Team in 2022

What is Traditional Publishing?

In traditional publishing, the author's work is submitted to a publisher, who then decides whether to publish the work. If the publisher decides to publish your book, they will typically enter into a contract with the author that outlines the terms of publication. The publisher will then print, distribute, and market the book. The traditional book publishing process typically requires an advance from the publisher to the author, which is an amount of money that is paid to the author upfront in order to secure the rights to publish their work. In return for this advance, the author agrees to give the publisher a percentage of the sales from the book. The traditional book publishing process is often seen as the more "traditional" or "established" way of getting a book published, and it is generally seen as being more difficult to get your book published through traditional means. However, traditional publishing can offer some advantages over self-publishing, such as higher quality standards, greater distribution and marketing reach, and more control over the final product.

History of Traditional Publishing

The history of traditional publishing is a long and winding road. It began with monks copying manuscripts by hand and then evolved into the printing press, which allowed for books to be printed in large quantities. This led to the establishment of publishing houses, which were responsible for distributing books to the masses. In the modern era, traditional publishing has come under fire from self-publishing and other forms of digital publishing, but it remains the most popular way to get a book published.

What is Self-Publishing?

Self-publishing is the process of independently publishing a book without the help of a traditional publishing house. This can be done through self-publishing platforms like Amazon Kindle Direct Publishing or through services like BookBaby or Ingram Spark.

Self-publishing has become increasingly popular in recent years, as more and more authors are choosing to take control of their own careers and publish their own books. There are many benefits to self-publishing, including the ability to keep 100% of the profits, the ability to control the book publishing process, and the ability to reach a wider audience.

There are also some drawbacks to self-publishing, including the fact that it can be expensive and time-consuming and that it can be difficult to get your book noticed in a crowded marketplace. But overall, self-publishing is a great option for authors who want to take control of their careers and publish their own books.

If you're interested in self-publishing, there are a few things you should keep in mind. First, make sure you have a great book that is ready to be published. Second, research the different self-publishing platforms and services available, and choose the one that's right for you. Third, create a professional-looking book cover and interior, and fourth, market your book effectively.

Self-publishing can be a great way to get your work out there into the world, and it can help you build a successful career as an author. If you're ready to take the plunge, there are a few things you should keep in mind to make sure your self-publishing experience is a positive one.

History of Self-Publishing

The history of self-publishing is a long and varied one, spanning centuries and encompassing a wide variety of formats and methods. The first instance of self-publishing is believed to date back to the 15th century when Johannes Gutenberg invented the printing press. This allowed for books to be printed and distributed more easily, paving the way for a wider range of titles to be made available to the public.

In more recent times, self-publishing has enjoyed a resurgence with the advent of technologies like the internet and e-books. This has allowed authors to publish their work without the need for a traditional publisher and has resulted in a greater diversity of titles being made available to readers.

There are now a number of different ways in which an author can self-publish their work, and the history of self-publishing is an ever-evolving one. With new technologies and methods being developed all the time, it seems likely that this trend will continue into the future, giving authors even more control over how their work is shared with the world.

Traditional Publishing vs. Self-Publishing

There are pros and cons to both traditional publishing and self-publishing. With traditional publishing, an author typically signs a contract with a publishing house, which agrees to publish the book. The publisher typically provides an advance against royalties and pays for the costs of printing and marketing the book. In return, the author typically gives the publisher exclusive rights to publish the book for a set period of time.

Self-publishing involves publishing the book oneself, either through a self-publishing company or by uploading the book to a self-publishing platform such as Amazon Kindle Direct Publishing. Authors who choose this route typically bear the full cost of printing and marketing your book. However, they also keep all profits from sales of the book.

Pros of Traditional Publishing

Traditional publishers have a well-established book publishing process for books, which can result in a higher quality product. They have a large pool of resources to draw on, including marketing and distribution channels that can help get a book out to a wider audience.

Publishers typically have more experience with the publishing industry and can offer helpful guidance and support throughout the book publishing process. Traditional publishers typically offer advances against royalties, which can provide some financial security for authors.

Most importantly, traditional publishers provide a level of credibility that can be invaluable for authors looking to establish themselves in the market.

Cons of Traditional Publishing

One of the cons of traditional publishing is that it can be very expensive. For example, if you want to publish your book through a traditional publisher, you might have to pay thousands of dollars for editing, design, and marketing services. Additionally, you will likely need to give up a significant amount of royalties (typically around 10-15%) to the publisher.

Another con of the traditional book publishing process is that it can be very slow. For example, it can take months or even years to get a book published through a traditional publisher. This is because traditional publishers typically only release a few books each year, and they need to go through a lengthy approval process before a book can be published.

Additionally, traditional publishers often have strict guidelines that authors must follow. For example, many traditional publishers require authors to use specific formatting for their manuscripts. This can make it difficult for authors to express their creativity in their writing.

Finally, traditional publishing can be risky for authors. This is because traditional publishers typically only accept a small percentage of the manuscripts that they receive. This means that there is a chance that an author could spend a lot of time and money on a book that never gets published.

Pros of Self-Publishing

There are several pros of self-publishing. First, you have complete creative control over your work. You make all the decisions about the content, the cover, the marketing, and everything else. This can be a pro or a con, depending on your point of view.Second, you keep all the profits from your book sales. There are no middlemen taking a cut of your profits.
Third, you can publish your book at your own pace. If you want to publish one book every year, you can do that. Or if you want to publish five books in six months, you can do that too.
Fourth, self-publishing gives you the opportunity to build an Author Platform. This is an important tool for any author, whether you want to traditionally publish your book or not. A platform is a way for you to reach your readers directly. It's a way to build a tribe of loyal followers who will buy anything you produce.

Last, self-publishing is simply an easier and faster process than traditional publishing. You don't have to go through the hassle of finding an agent or a publisher. You can upload your book to Amazon and other online retailers and start selling immediately.

Cons of Self-Publishing

One of the cons of self-publishing is that it can be expensive. You need to invest in your own editing, cover design, and marketing. Additionally, you need to make sure you have an audience interested in your book before you publish it. If you don't have a following or a platform, it can be difficult to generate interest in your book.

Another con of self-publishing is that you don't have the same level of control over the final product. You're responsible for everything from editing to marketing, and if something goes wrong, it's on you to fix it. This can be a lot of pressure, and it's not for everyone.

Finally, self-publishing can be a bit of a gamble. There's no guarantee that your book will be successful, no matter how good it is. Even if you do everything right, there's always a chance that your book will flop. If you're not prepared for this possibility, you might want to reconsider self-publishing.

These are just a few of the cons of self-publishing. It's important to weigh all the pros and cons before you make a decision about whether or not to self-publish your book.
